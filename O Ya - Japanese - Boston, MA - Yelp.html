        Listed in Eat or Die, 2015 Yelp Reviews!

        My husband took me to O Ya for my birthday. I had read so many reviews, that no restaurant could be as good as O Ya was in my mind.

        At any rate, I got reservations by calling about a month in advance. I put down a credit card and was told that there was a $75/ person charge for no shows. The restaurant is quite small with tables and seats at the bar where you can watch the action.If you are standing facing the sign, the unmarked entrance is to your left.

        The hostess was very nice, seating us early, when we arrived, and taking our coats. They remember your coats without giving you a number. That is class.

        I was torn between the larger Grand Omakase menu, where the courses were listed, and the less expensive chef's tasting menu. I wanted to do wine and sake pairings, which were $80 on the chef's tasting menu and $150 on the Grand Omakase menu, and that price difference pushed me over the edge. The chef's tasting menu had less items and they aren't listed, but you can specify preferences and allergies. I was so full after eating it, I can't imagine eating more.

        My husband ordered ala carte, and the portions are a bit larger, like two pieces of nigiri instead one one per plate.

        Our waitress was outstanding. She asked how she should time bringing out plates since my husband was getting less, and we just asked for them as they came, which was fine.

        The ambience is hipster chic. We dressed up but you could go in jeans. I LOVED the music, catching everything from Hendrix to Iggy Pop. You don't expect to hear something off of Axis: Bold as Love being played at a restaurant often listed as one of the ritziest in Boston.

        I didn't take notes so I can't describe everything I had. I don't generally like sake, but the sake I had here was quite good. As I suspected, I just don't like the cheap saki I have had before. My favorite had a pine note, almost like Greek retsina as a saki. The waitress thoughtfully offered to do all wine if I didn't like the saki.

        Some of things I liked included (I think):

        Warm braised shiitake mushroom, anise hysop, truffle honey sauce. Rich and delicious.

        Soy marinated salmon, truffle oil, green onion. Hard to go wrong there.

        Warm chive blossom omelette, sweet dashi sauce, shiso. Tiny and adorable.

        Homemade fingerling potato chip, perigord truffle. Tiny potato chip on sushi rice, delicious and rich.

        Sake braised short ribs (or some kind of short rib). This was awesome and I don't even like red meat much. The most tender beef I have ever encountered. Someone must have massaged that cow within an inch of his or her life.

        Chilled maine lobster salad, avocado, creamy yuzu dressing,micro greens, cucumber gelee. I snagged a bite from my husband. Tasty.

        I also ordered the aged sake gelato, financier, vanilla cremeaux, miso-hazelnut caramel for dessert. Amazingly I, a member of the clean plate club, couldn't finish it, I was so full!

        And here's a few I really didn't like:

        Foie gras, balsamic chocolate kabayaki, claudio corallo raisin cocoa pulp, sip of aged sake. This just tasted like a mouthful of fat to me.

        Sea urchin. I'm just not a fan at all.

        I do think there was an over abundance of torching and truffle oil as others have mentioned. I love raw sushi.

        The wine and saki pairings come in 3 oz. glasses and I think I may have had 7? I was quite drunk by the time we left. My husband didn't drink alcohol and was sad they didn't have coffee.

        Check your check, a 20% gratuity was added.

        We went home and we had a lovely time.

        Unfortunately, I got ill during the night. I blamed the alcohol but my husband had the same symptoms. I know it's very hard to pinpoint if a particular food or restaurant made you ill, but I did call the restaurant and gave them a head's up.

        ***O Ya perfectly combines Japanese cuisine with contemporary French fine dining to deliver a one-of-a-kind dining experience***

        O Ya is not simply another high-end sushi restaurant even though some may describe it that way.  O Ya's sushi preparations are far more complex than what you would find at an authentic omakase restaurant like Sushi Zo or even a more fusion sushi spot like Nobu.  While the flavor, freshness and quality of the fish is maintained, O Ya's sushi and sashimi courses are topped with premium ingredients like truffle as well as ingredients like banana peppers that you would rarely find at a Japanese restaurant.  Some of the seafood is served raw, but quite a few of the fish courses are seared to provide a more satisfying textural contrast; they are then dressed in sauces that are very indulgent as ingredients like uni and truffle are paired.

        O Ya's omakase is expensive, but it unveils one perfect plate after another as a wide variety of seafood as well as luxurious proteins are utilized.  Nigiri is really done well here.  The hamachi with banana pepper mousse and truffle is exceptional.  The searing of the hamachi not only provides a great crispy crust against a warm, moist and soft center, but it warms up the banana pepper mousse and truffle to unleash an aroma that gives off heat as well as the truffle's earthiness at the same time--a rare combination that works.  Warm snapper is dressed with a sweet and tangy plum vinaigrette and fragrant shiso.  A lightly spicy fried oyster on top of rice is finished off with creamy, citrusy aioli and squid ink bubbles while high quality foie gras (creamy and rich) is garnished with dark chocolate and sweet and sour aged balsamic.

        There are excellent sashimi courses too.  Some are refreshing like the fatty bluefin toro topped with wasabi oil and crisp scallions.  Their Japanese sea bream has more of a bite over the soft toro, but the garnishes are also refreshing and vibrant--white soy, ginger and lemon oil.  Aji is prepared Peruvian, ceviche style with an acidic leche de tigre and fragrant cilantro.

        The chefs also prepare indulgent sashimi courses too.  Unbelievably soft diver scallop is served with fragrant shaved truffle and a jus that is thin and clean but also exceptionally rich and creamy as uni is paired with truffle.  It is the type of sauce that you would want to use almost everywhere.

        There are many other cold and hot preparations with their omakase.  A raw kumamoto oyster is perfectly paired with sweet and refreshing watermelon pearls as well as vinegary and refreshing cucumber mignonette.  Moist ocean trout and exploding with juiciness trout roe are wrapped in cucumber.  A spoon of crispy and crunchy puffed foie gras with yuzu and miso delivers a cereal-like texture as well as a strong, meaty and rich foie gras flavor.

        Hot preparations reveal a lot of skill in the kitchen.  Spring asparagus risotto is topped with nicely grated cured egg yolk.  Mushrooms are simply prepared but then topped with a nutty sesame froth.  Warm lobster is topped with equal shavings of truffle and bonito and the taste alternates between fragrant and earthy to salty and fishy.  A rich and soft slow cooked egg reveals a rich and creamy egg yolk center while the egg white becomes almost custard-like; the egg is garnished with caviar and savory dashi.  Their BBQ rib has a sweet and salty glaze as well as truly fall off the bone tender meat while their Kobe Wagyu strip loin is well marbled with a soft, fatty and almost melted center; the beef is served with a side of crispy potatoes layered with black truffle.

        Desserts are simple but nicely presented and reveal clean yet rich flavors like fragile white chocolate coins that release liquid chocolate ganache; the chocolate coins are served in chilled, refreshing and slightly flavored milk.  The chocolates closing the dinner are beautiful and showcase Japanese flavors.

        Service is exceptional.  The food comes out at a perfect pace and my server Anna-Li was equal parts friendly and polite as well as very polished and extremely knowledgeable about the menu and the preparations--one of the friendliest servers I have ever had at a high-end restaurant.

        O Ya has a dark but nice dining room.  With the dark lighting, the great dark wood and simple space, it feels like an intimate and hidden upscale bar/lounge.

        O Ya is a very expensive restaurant, but it would easily be among the very best restaurants in SF, NY or Chicago.

        O Ya is a perfect blending of Japanese cuisine and high-end, contemporary fine dining tasting menus.  If you enjoy both Japanese food and fine dining and find yourself in Boston for either a short or long stay, O Ya is a must visit.

        I am not sure if street parking is available.  There is a nearby pay parking structure that is expensive.

        ROTD 12/11/2014

        An Experience You Will Not Soon Forget!

        O Ya is just one of those things that you need to experience to understand.  If you read the reviews here, you will find two things are consistent: first, the food is innovative, delicious, and mind-blowing and second, it's expensive.  Let's start with the second observation. Yes, dinner at O Ya will run you.  If you do the Omakase, you can expect to pay around $200 dollars per person at least (the Grand Omakase even more).  If you do the ala carte menu, you won't get some of the Omakase-limited selections but you have complete control of what you choose.  With that control, you can try lots of things and keep the meal from getting outrageously expensive.  I have done both and can confidently tell you that an ala carte strategy is the way to go.  Why?  Because the menu is enormous and if you know a little bit about things you like and don't like, you can "journey" through the ala carte menu and not worry about getting something you don't care for.

        The ambiance of the restaurant is simplistic but very engaging.  You feel like you are in a secret kitchen.  The door to the restaurant is nondescript.  The darkness focuses you on the food.  Sit at the bar if you can.  You will enjoy watching the master chefs prepare each order with delicately and meticulously.  The service is excellent and you will be well cared for.  They offer valet parking or if lucky there are street spots.

        Let's talk about the drinks first.  You can choose wine pairings, sake, tea, etc.  It is all excellent and your server can help you choose.

        For the dinner, here's a breakdown:
        Seared marinated shitake mushroom nigiri- delicious sweet and salty plump shitake rebounds its taste off the slightly malty rice
        Fried Kumamoto oyster nigiri- have to get this.  Hint of wasabi, malty briny taste of the fried oyster, and the accent of the foam.  Do not miss this.
        Torched duck sashimi- the fat rim of the duck breaks down from the heat and add salt to the slightly sweet duck.  Another favorite.
        Okinawan marinated pork- served in a sweet and salty broth.  Delicious.
        Ika nigiri- Torched squid with uni butter, microbeans, and shiso.  The hit of the night.  We got two orders.  Butter, salty, chewy, and slightly sweet.  The uni provides the base of the flavor and it is outstanding.
        Kumamoto oysters raw with watermelon pearls and cucumber- refreshing, briny, slightly sweet.  Perfection.
        Venison torched with porcini crema and ponzu oil- slightly gamey, smooth from the cream, outstanding.
        Soy marinated salmon nigiri- delicate, slightly salty, and tender buttery flesh.
        Bluefin maguro nigiri-  fresh tuna lays the dancefloor and the foie gras ponzu dances all over it.
        Chopped tea brined chicken thighs- cucumber, avocado, carrot contrast the sweet chicken.  A favorite.
        Warm somen noodles- with an slowly cooked onsen egg and delicate big fin Japanese squid tempura.  Spicy so watch out!
        Foie gras gyoza- creamy with a pink peppercorn for zest!
        Shrimp tempura- in a sweet salty broth.  The malty shrimp melts in your mouth.  You can pretty much eat everything except the tail!
        Chicken broth with foie gras shumai- slightly tangy broth with delicate shitake mushrooms.  Very hearty.

        And for dessert:
        Molten chocolate pudding cake- delicate smooth, with shiso marinated cherries and creme fraiche.  A nice way to cap off the meal.

        They will also bring you some delicious house chocolates with the bill!  White chocolate with green tea truffle inside.  Wow!

        O Ya is perfect for that special occasion and for the special someone who can appreciate the artistry (the food is as pleasing visually as it is tasting).  Those who have not experimented much with Japanese cuisine may find it overwhelming.  

        If it's helpful for Yelpers, a couple eating from the ala carte menu can expect to spend around $180-200 including a modest drink tab.  A couple that really wants to experiment with the menu can expect to pay around $250-300 including a modest drink tab.  Good tip included.

        It is a treat and the memory of the meal will last much longer than the sting of the bill.  Oh yeah!
