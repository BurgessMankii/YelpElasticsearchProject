        Pros:
        + Best oysters
        + Amazing lobster rolls
        + Yummy uni toast
        + Great food in general
        + Great service (see below)

        Cons:
        - Long wait because they're too popular and don't take reservations

        Came here for dinner with my fiancée and my sister on a Thursday night. We arrived at around 7:30pm and were told that it would be about 1-1.5 hour wait. They took our name and number and we went to a coffee shop nearby until they called us about an hour later.

        We ordered the Neptune Plateau ($99) which is basically a giant sampler of oysters, clams, shrimp, lobster cocktail, crab, etc. The food, especially the oysters, was so fresh. The sauces made everything extra flavorful. In addition, before the seafood came, we ordered Uni Toast ($4), which is basically toast with uni (sea urchin) butter. It was savory and opened up my appetite for the seafood to come. We also ordered a lobster roll ($27) to share, which was absolutely delicious.

        This was my third time having raw oysters, and by far the oysters here were the best - they were the most fresh and flavorful oysters I've ever had.

        Also, the service here is great. Our server accidentally knocked an oyster out of our platter, so they replaced it later with two oysters!

        My only complaint is that the restaurant is pretty small, very popular, and doesn't take reservations, so there will inevitably be a long wait. But I felt it was well worth the wait.

        Listed in All Things Boston Area, North End, Boston

        I'm really torn between four and five stars here. I think the 4.5 average star rating Neptune Oyster currently has is pretty darn accurate. When planning a trip to Boston, I went to Yelp to explore the types of great food that I just "had" to try and Neptune Oyster kept coming up.

        We decided to put our name in at around 5:15 PM on a Saturday after seeing a play in the theater district. It was very cramped/crowded inside. This is a small place and I can see exactly why there is always such a long wait for a table here, even if it is also supposed to be very high quality. When we walked in, we could barely even budge because there were some people waiting for a table. It seems that they had been called letting them know their table was ready but that it actually wasn't quite ready. I later learned that they call you when the check gets sent out to a table, but that sometimes people take longer to pay/leave. A group cut in front of us, put their name in, and the hostess went away. It took a minute to get her attention because there's no designated hostess stand (I guess there isn't room for it). We were quoted a one hour wait.

        We enjoyed exploring North End (including a stop at Mike's Pastry), then we were called after exactly an hour had passed. When we arrived back at Neptune Oyster, our table was ready and we were seated immediately. We were seated at the end of a six-person table which was occupied by four guys. We were in such close proximity to them that it almost felt awkward to have any other conversation. We could hear everything they said; we were all at the same table, after all! In the end, the guys ended up just including us in their conversation for the rest of our meal, including inviting us to add our phones to their pile. It ended up enhancing the meal, making it more fun, but it could have been really awkward. This was supposed to be a "sister date," but there's nothing at all intimate about dining at Neptune Oyster.

        I'm a recent college grad and my sister is a college student, so we ended up deciding to split the lobster roll ($27). We were nervous our waiter wouldn't allow it or make us feel guilty, but he was totally understanding about it. We ordered it hot with butter. They cut it in half, gave us two plates, and two ketchup containers. That was very thoughtful! There was also no extra cost for splitting the plate. The lobster was VERY fresh and slightly stringy. I got a little piece of lobster stuck in my teeth at some point. It was perfect hot with the butter, although I'm interested what the cold mayo tastes like (maybe if I ever have it during the hotter months). The fries were good, but a little salty for my liking--nothing special. The lobster roll was the clear standout.

        There isn't enough space to wait by the bathroom with servers going from behind the bar to the kitchen, etc. I tried to wait, but then ended up waiting at the table while keeping my eye on the bathroom door.

        I DEFINITELY recommend Neptune Oyster for the lobster roll, as long as you're aware it's small, not fancy atmosphere with quite the fancy prices, and there will be a wait (no reservations). If you're aware of that/prepared for it going in, you'll definitely walk away a fan like I did.

        Good things come to those who wait. And wait you shall. I arrived at 9:30 PM on a Saturday night and waited 15 minutes to be seated. I consider our wait time to be a black swan event primarily because my small party of me, myself, and my plus one could have fit anywhere. To put things in perspective, a party of 4 on their way out, informed us they waited an hour and 45 minutes. The homey place does not take reservations by phone but if you come in person, give your name, and number, you can wander around Hanover St whilst you wait. Or play Clash of Clans.

        The oysters were very tasty and true to their descriptions. I am very glad they included a detailed description of the flavors to expect from each different oyster. When they say "high salt," they are being precise. My favorite was the Kumamoto, a low salt yet very full tasting oyster - the salinity was just right, although the serving size was on the small side. My least favorite was the Kusshi, I am not a fan of the cucumber taste and my naivety thought otherwise. Do.Not.Ignore.Their.Descriptions. The oysters are cleaned and shucked gracefully in front of you, no sand, no remnants, no complaints.

        The lobster roll comes in two styles, warm and buttered up or cold and loaded with mayo. We went for the butter. The roll was toasty and crunchy, and the lobs were large, chunky, and very fulfilling. There is a fair distribution of knuckle and tail, so no bias to lobster body parts. The ratio of lobster to bread is perfect, no more, no less.

        Overall, a great place to eat with one or two other people. Any groups larger than 5 should expect lengthy wait times. Also, parking in this area was a hassle. The snow did not help, nor did the Bruins game. I would recommend getting a cab next time. Or a rickshaw.

But along with top marks from a certain crowd-sourced review site I happen to hold in high esteem, the majority of my peers recommended Neptune Oyster. I was not led astray.

        Even with a meeting that ran long, my driver hitting some traffic on the way into town (business obligations were actually 45 minutes outside the city), and a short wait for a seat upon arriving, I made it happen. I sat at the long, packed bar by myself, spent a solid half-hour enjoying some of Neptune Oyster's most popular menu items, threw back a couple Massachusetts beers (I see you, Cape Ann Fisherman's Brew), and chatted up the bartender/waitress about Boston in general, all with time to spare before my short shuttle flight back home.

        I basically Bourdained the shit outta this place.

        Seriously, if you find yourself in the same situation, Neptune Oyster is a perfect place to grab a bite by yourself. The aforementioned bar takes up half the room (and the room is small to begin with), so requesting a single stool seems pretty standard.

        The service was delightful, attentive, and on point. When I asked if I should get my Maine Lobster Roll hot or cold, I was immediately told hot, without a shred of doubt.

        What my server failed to mention was that this was easily the biggest lobster roll on the planet. Even at $27, you're totally getting your money's worth - just ginormous chunks of hot lobster, with a simple coating of butter (I could've gone with more of a dousing, but maybe that's just me), on a lightly toasted roll. We're talkin' a two-hander here. Not counting the giant heaping of fries that comes with it. Maybe not the absolute best lobster roll I've ever had, but still very much up there, and without question, the most monstrous.

        I also ordered the Wellfleet Clam Chowder because, like the lobster roll, this seemed like the right move while in New England. I typically like my clam chowder a little thicker, but the flavor here was great, and didn't warrant the extra cream. The potatoes, however, were noticeably undercooked.

        Minor nitpicking aside (and that's all that it is, because this place is fantastic), Neptune Oyster is seafood central for all the right reasons. The tiny restaurant is bustling, the tiled walls and pressed-tin ceilings possess old-school charm, the staff is friendly and convincing, the food is fresh and generously portioned, and it's all of a ten minute taxi ride from Logan. Add 'em all up and I think what I'm trying to say is... I'm about to schedule these one-day business trips to the Boston area more often.
